Work

  • 2018 - 2024
    Research Assistant
    RF/Analog Research Group, Texas Tech University
    Developing and testing passive radar systems operating in the 5.8-GHz ISM band for noncontact motion and target detection leveraging ambient wireless signals. Analyzed the limitations of passive radar systems and proposed methods of maximizing the sensing ability. Developed passive radar beamforming theory using signal propagation and target models and experimentally verified the efficacy of the model using a custom-designed 16-channel beamforming passive radar. Guided visiting high school student’s experiments on using passive sensors to monitor room occupancy.

    Developed and experimentally verified new FMCW radar sensing architectures for dynamic analog clutter removal using feedforward cancellation leveraging microwave passive components. Developing novel circuits to create programmable true-time delay for FMCW clutter cancellation.

    Designed high-voltage analog CMOS circuits after implementing a private Cadence Custom IC design server to programmatically drive a 32x32 optical phased array with µm-scale pitch. Developed high-efficiency optical, phased array, and semiconductor simulations using COMSOL.

    Designed and analyzed a novel microwave pulse oximetry system and its interactions with biological tissue in COMSOL and unified FEM results with known radar signal models.

Publications

  • 2024.05
    Low-Cost Fast-Start Amplifier Architecture for AC-Coupled Measurement of Low-Frequency Signals
    IEEE Sensors Letters
    This paper presents a low-cost baseband amplifier which uses two commercial diodes to accomplish fast start. Compared to the state-of-the-art, the proposed amplifier is considerably simpler than DC-coupled solutions and uses only two additional components to provide a greatly accelerated startup time.
  • 2024.01
    A Digital Beamforming Approach for Indoor Passive Sensing
    2024 IEEE Topical Conference on Wireless Sensors and Sensor Networks
    This paper presents a digital beamforming model that can be used with passive radar sensors. The theory is first developed and verified through an RF time-domain and closed-form baseband simulation, and is then verified by a 2-channel passive sensor. Results show good agreement with expected values, setting a foundation for future works.
  • 2024.01
    Secure Occupancy Sensing with Passive Radar for Spectrally Congested Spaces
    2024 IEEE Topical Conference on Wireless Sensors and Sensor Networks
    This paper, written in collaboration with a visiting high-school researcher, studies the potential to use passive radar in a secure occupancy sensing application. Results show good potential for use in an undetectable occupancy sensor.
  • 2024
    Reconfigurable FMCW Resolution Improvement Using Adaptive Cancellation
    IEEE Microwave and Wireless Technology Letters
    This paper proposes a feedforward cancellation approach to static clutter cancellation in FMCW radar. Experimental results are compared against the developed theory, which shows potential to remove the impacts of stationary clutter in FMCW sensing.
  • 2024
    A Digital Beamforming Fast-Start Passive Radar for Indoor Motion Detection and Angle Estimation
    IEEE Transactions on Microwave Theory and Techniques
    This work presents a 16-channel passive sensor that uses digital beamforming and an integrated fast-start amplifier to accomplish motion detection and angle estimation using ubiquitous wireless signals. The results of this work show good agreement with proposed signal models and simulations, and showed potential in detecting small motions such as human vital signs.
  • 2023.12
    Recent Progress in Passive Sensing Leveraging Advanced Circuit Techniques
    2023 Asia-Pacific Microwave Conference
    This work reviews recent advances in passive sensing in automotive applications using novel antenna arrays, where a nonuniform linear array can be used alongside Doppler ambiguity removal to localize targets. In addition, passive radar motion sensors are reviewed including interferometric approaches and self-injection-locked approaches.
  • 2023.05
    Null/Optimum Point Optimization for Indoor Passive Radar Motion Sensing
    2023 IEEE Radar Conference
    This study further develops passive sensing signal models to analyze the impacts of null-points on motion sensing. Analysis revealed elliptically distributed null points around the sensor-transmitter pair. Using frequency- and spatially-diverse systems is revealed to offer a route to improved coverage using simple passive sensors.
  • 2023.01
    Passive Multistatic Wireless Sensing Based on Discrete LNA/Mixer Co-Optimization and Fast-Startup Baseband Amplifier
    2023 IEEE Topical Conference on Wireless Sensors and Sensor Networks
    This paper presents a passive sensor operating in the 5.8-GHz Wi-Fi band which uses a custom LNA and mixer alongside a fast-startup baseband amplifier. Experimental results show the ability to precisely detect mm-scale motion frequencies.
  • 2021.11
    A Novel Wavelength-Division Differential Detection Technique for Microwave Pulse Oximetry
    2021 Annual International Conference of the IEEE Engineering in Medicine and Biology Society (EMBC)
    This paper proposes a novel wavelength-division technique to accomplish microwave pulse oximetry. A full-wave simulation using COMSOL Multiphysics is carried out to quantify the performance of the sensor, while a MATLAB simulation is used to create an example time-domain response.
